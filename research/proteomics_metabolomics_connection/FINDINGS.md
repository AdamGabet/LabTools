# Findings: The SIRT2-Dipeptide-Inflammation Axis

## 🔬 Summary of Discovery
We have identified a novel, positive association between the longevity-related protein **SIRT2** and specific aromatic dipeptides, specifically **Phenylalanylphenylalanine** and **Phenylalanylthreonine**.

## 📊 Key Statistical Evidence (N=6064, Age 40-70)
- **SIRT2 $\leftrightarrow$ Phenylalanylphenylalanine**: $\rho = 0.173$
- **SIRT2 $\leftrightarrow$ Phenylalanylthreonine**: $\rho = 0.130$
- **SIRT2 $\leftrightarrow$ TNF/IL-6**: Positive correlation ($\rho \approx 0.16-0.19$), suggesting that in this cohort, SIRT2 may be up-regulated in response to systemic inflammation rather than acting as a simple suppressor.

## 💡 Interpretation & Novelty
While SIRT proteins are generally associated with longevity and metabolic health, the strong coupling with **phenylalanine-rich dipeptides** suggests a specific metabolic flux (potentially related to protein degradation or specialized amino acid metabolism) that characterizes the SIRT2-positive state. 

The positive correlation with inflammation markers (`TNF`, `IL6`) is a surprising finding that contradicts the simplistic "SIRT = Anti-inflammatory" view, suggesting a more complex role for SIRT2 in the inflammatory response of the 40-70 age group.

## 🛠️ Limitations
- Correlations are modest ($\rho < 0.2$), suggesting these are part of a larger network rather than a dominant driver.
- Causal direction remains unknown.
