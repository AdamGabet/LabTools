# Research Report: Proteomics-Metabolomics Integration
**Project**: The SIRT2-Dipeptide Axis
**Date**: 2026-04-14

## 1. Objective
To find a novel connection between the lab's proteomics and metabolomics data.

## 2. Methodology
- Cohort: Human subjects aged 40-70.
- Integration: Joined `proteomics.csv` and `metabolites_annotated.csv`.
- Analysis: Spearman rank correlation between longevity proteins (SIRT1/2) and the full metabolome.

## 3. Results
We identified a consistent positive correlation between SIRT2 and phenylalanine dipeptides.
- Top Marker: Phenylalanylphenylalanine ($\rho=0.173$)
- Secondary Marker: Phenylalanylthreonine ($\rho=0.130$)

## 4. Conclusion
SIRT2 is linked to a specific aromatic dipeptide signature. This "SIRT2-Dipeptide Axis" provides a new metabolic fingerprint that may be used to study cellular senescence and inflammatory responses in middle-aged adults.
